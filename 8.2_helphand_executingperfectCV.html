Executing the perfect
cover letter

The job market is so incredibly tough in this day and age that trying to stay ahead
is so head. So executing your Cover Letter perfectly is crucially beneficial to being
one step closer to securing a job.

We have listed a few things that we feel are important to keep in mind when you
are creating your work of art:

  • Always have a persona that you actually are

    Let me explain, I know we all like to use massive words that make us sound smart
    however if you sit in front of a panel of people and you speak the complete opposite
    of how you write its not going to be a happy ending.

    So what we recommend is to use a writing style that you are comfortable with and
    that portrays the correct image of the person you really are.

  • how can you help me?

    We its true that having a transferable skill set is a plus for an employer, use sales for
    example if they see that you have gained skills within the sales environment then
    they will look to see how those skills can be adapted into their business
    to get more bang for their buck.

    So what we are saying is that pointing out skills that you feel can be attractively
    admired     by a future employer is in your best interest, and if you have that hidden
    gem waiting to be discovered then I actively encourage to come forth and flaunt it,
    you never know how desired that skill may be... to someone else.

  • Being assertive at the end

    I would always look to extend that traditional "I look forward to hearing from you
    in the near future" Because they hear that six hundred times a day what they really
    want is why should they call you. "I believe I can be a valuable member to the team
    and I am really looking forward to hearing from you soon."

    Just by adding that at the end is showing a higher level of interest instead of just
    closing off that with a boring traditional way less normal the better.
