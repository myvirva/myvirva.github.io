HOW TO WRITE A PERFECT
RESUME / CV.

So you have come for some advice? Well you have come to the right place!
(GET IT) What do you want to know? We have a structure below that may help
you however if it doesn't then we are happy to answer any question you may have!

Writing a CV/Resume can be daunting and hard to do, to execute a perfect
CV / Resume you want to make sure you keep in mind the following things:


  • You're real...so sound real!

    A CV is document that is generally no longer then
    2x A4 page of information about yourself, you are
    not writing the next Harry Potter book so keep it real
    and short and sweet.

    If it's too long they wont read it!

  • The difference between a tailored
    suit and normal one?

    We are all guilty of just whipping put CV's Left,
    Right and Center to try and apply for as many jobs
    as possible.
    Let me ask you something, How's that
    going for you?
    Take the time to address each job in your CV
    and tweak your skill set around the
    description criteria for the job,
    Just remember if you take the time so will they.

  • Give it personality!

    There is nothing worse then reading a CV with no
    heart its like reading a recipe book (No
    subtance and plan boring) give it some flair
    add a personal statement that sums you up in
    2-3 sentences (what are your likes?).

  • Been out of the game a while?
    be honest, add a positive twist.

    When a potential employer or Recruitment
    consultant is reading your CV / Resume
    and there is obvious gaps throughout, br it is going to catch their eye. They are going to
    not give you the benefit of the doubt necessarily.
    So if you stopped work for something adds a
    little note explaining the circumstances
    around it.

  • Spell check isn't your friend
    once it's sent.

    It's an error we have all made at some point sending
    out something that is clearly got mistakes all through it.
    Let me tell you that YES! They do look to see those and
    they find any excuse to chuck you on the NO pile.
    So make sure you go over it and even get a friend or
    someone around you to read it because a second pair
    of eyes never hurt anyone.

  • True or False.

    You are as transparent as clear film to some
    people, make sure everything you put in your
    CV is correct     and that when you go into
    an interview situation you know the
    contents     of your document.

  • Out with the old in with
    the new.

    Keeping your information current and up to date
    is a mandatory requirement in the game, lik they
    say would you like to spend 30 seconds once a
    month or a week at the end of the year?
    Moral or the story keep it super current and
    up to date

  • Stand out from the rest.

    We live in one of those times that you aren't any
    more important then the next person walking
    down the road, however its all about presentation
    so if your going to do it make it look really good
    and stand out from the rest.

  • keywords are your best friend

    These days most recruitment companies use
    software that scans out the keywords in your CV
    so make sure that when applying for those
    jobs or up loading any candidate information
    that the keywords that any employer will be
    searching is included somewhere in your
    CV/Resume.
